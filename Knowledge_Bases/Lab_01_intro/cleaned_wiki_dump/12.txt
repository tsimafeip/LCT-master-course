'Serena Jameka Williams' (born September 26, 1981)  name "WTA profile" /> is an American professional tennis player. She has been ranked List of WTA number 1 ranked singles tennis players singles world No. 1 by Women's Tennis Association (WTA) for 319 weeks, including a joint record 186 consecutive weeks, and finished as year end No. 1 five times. She has won 23 Grand Slam (tennis)#Tournaments Grand Slam singles titles, most by any player in Open Era, and All time tennis records – women's singles#Grand Slam singles totals second most of all time (behind Margaret Court's 24).

Along with her older sister Venus Williams Venus, Serena Williams was coached by her parents Oracene Price and Richard Williams (tennis coach) Richard Williams. Turning professional in 1995, she won her first major singles title at 1999 US Open – Women's singles 1999 US Open. From 2002 French Open – Women's singles 2002 French Open to 2003 Australian Open – Women's singles 2003 Australian Open, she was dominant, winning all four major singles titles (each time over Venus in final) to achieve a Grand Slam (tennis)#Non calendar year Grand Slam non calendar year Grand Slam and Grand Slam (tennis)#Career Grand Slam career Grand Slam, known as "Serena Slam". The next few years saw her claim two more singles majors, but suffer from injury and decline in form. Beginning in 2007, however, she gradually returned to form despite continued injuries, retaking world No. 1 singles ranking. Records
Williams has won a record 13 Grand Slam singles titles on hard court. She holds Open Era record for most women's singles titles at Australian Open (7) and shares with Chris Evert Open Era record for most titles won at US Open (tennis) US Open (6). She also holds records for most women's singles matches won at majors (365) and most singles majors won since turning 30 years old (10). She is only tennis player, male or female, to win three of four Grand Slams at least 6 times. She is also a five time winner of WTA Tour Championships in singles division. > web date September 10, 2012 title Serena, Liezel & Lisa, Andrea & Lucie Qualify url 13, 2012 access date June 18, 2013 website Women's Tennis Association WTA >

In doubles, Williams sisters have third most women's doubles Grand Slam titles, behind 18 titles of Natasha Zvereva (14 with Gigi Fernández) and record 20 titles won by Martina Navratilova and Pam Shriver. Serena and Venus are only tennis players in history with four Olympic gold medals (three in women's doubles together and one each in singles), and to win Olympic gold in same event on three occasions. They are only Open Era women tennis players to win Olympic Gold in both singles and doubles categories.  name "CBS Olympic"> news  date August 5, 2012 title Venus and Serena Williams win Olympic gold  url London, England work  CBS News agency Associated Press access date April 24, 2017 > At 2012 Summer Olympics, Serena Williams became only third player to win Olympic gold medals in both singles and doubles at one Olympic Games, after Helen Wills Moody at 1924 Summer Olympics and Venus at 2000 Summer Olympics 2000 Sydney Olympics. Williams is only player, male or female, to accomplish a Grand Slam (tennis) Career Golden Slam in both singles and doubles. > web Berkok John date 4 August 2020 title On This Day  Serena completes her Golden Slam at 2012 London Olympics url July 2021 website Tennis.com language en > > web title Olympedia – Olympians Who Won a Golden Slam in Tennis url June 2021 website www.olympedia.org >
