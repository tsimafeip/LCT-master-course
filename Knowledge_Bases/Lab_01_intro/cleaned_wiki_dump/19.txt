The second draft of Articles of Confederation, prepared by John Dickinson and completed no later than June&nbsp;17, 1776, declared "The name of this Confederation shall be 'United States of America'."sfn Safire 2003 p 199 The final version of Articles, sent to states for ratification in late 1777, stated that "The Stile of this Confederacy shall be 'The United States of America'."sfn Mostert 2005 p 18 In June 1776, Thomas Jefferson wrote phrase "UNITED STATES OF AMERICA" in all capitalized letters in headline of his "original Rough draught" of United States Declaration of Independence Declaration of Independence.sfn Safire 2003 p 199 This draft of document did not surface until June&nbsp;21, 1776, and it is unclear whether it was written before or after Dickinson used term in his June 17 draft of Articles of Confederation.sfn Safire 2003 p 199

The phrase "United States" was originally plural in American usage. It described a collection of states—e.g., "United States are..." The singular form became popular after end of American Civil War Civil War and is now standard usage. A Citizenship of United States citizen of United States is an "Americans American". "United States", "American", and "U.S." refer to country adjectivally ("American values", "U.S.&nbsp;forces").
